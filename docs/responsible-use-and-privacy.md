# Responsible Use and Privacy

GEAkr is a lightweight folder pattern for organizing project context. It does not replace Government of Canada guidance, departmental policy, privacy review, security review, or user responsibility when using artificial intelligence tools.

## Responsible use

Users should follow applicable Government of Canada and organizational guidance for the responsible use of artificial intelligence tools. This includes using AI in a way that is transparent, accountable, secure, privacy-aware, and appropriate for the sensitivity of the information involved.

When using GEAkr with a chatbot or AI assistant, users should consider:

- whether the information is appropriate to use with the selected AI tool
- whether the AI tool is approved or acceptable for the intended use
- whether the information includes personal, sensitive, confidential, protected, regulated, or restricted content
- whether the output will be used for advice, decision support, publication, operational action, or internal drafting
- whether human review is required before relying on or sharing the output

## Privacy boundary

GEAkr does not create a privacy boundary. The privacy boundary is determined by the storage location, chatbot provider, connectors, permissions, user settings, and applicable organizational rules.

Content may be transmitted to a chatbot provider when a user:

- pastes text into a chatbot
- uploads a file
- connects a storage location such as SharePoint, Google Drive, Dropbox, or Git
- allows a chatbot or assistant to read files through a connector
- allows a chatbot or assistant to write generated content back to storage

Users should understand where information is being sent, what provider terms apply, and whether provider settings allow content to be used for service improvement or model training.

## Commercial chatbot providers

When using commercial chatbot services, users remain responsible for reviewing the provider’s terms of use, privacy notice, retention practices, data-use settings, and any available controls related to model training or service improvement.

Where available, users should review settings that control whether conversations, uploaded files, or connected content may be used to improve the provider’s models or services.

## Self-hosted or open-source tools

If using a self-hosted or open-source assistant, the boundary is different. Data handling depends on the local deployment, model runtime, indexing pipeline, logs, storage, authentication, infrastructure, and administrative controls.

Self-hosted does not automatically mean private or safe. The deployment still needs appropriate controls.

## Suggested safe-use rule

Do not place sensitive, personal, protected, confidential, regulated, or restricted information into a GEAkr folder unless the selected storage location, chatbot, connector, permissions, and organizational approvals are appropriate for that information.

## Plain-language summary

GEAkr helps organize what a chatbot should consider. It does not decide what information is safe to share. Users must apply the applicable Government of Canada guidance, organizational rules, privacy obligations, provider terms, and tool settings before using project material with an AI assistant.
